Im a food expert - the 6 products that seem healthy but arent that you should avoid at Aldi

NOT everything is a healthy buy at Aldi.

Although the grocery chain is widely considered the cheapest in the country, some products may lack in taste quality.

Or worse yet, some items might appear healthy – but might not actually be.

Luckily, TikToker whitesugarrebel1, who often creates content that educates her fans on food products, has revealed a roundup of the most deceiving items at Aldi.

Here are the products she recommends avoiding because they aren't completely healthy.

1. Simply Nature Seedtastic bread

Firstly, you need to be aware of the bread you are buying.

READ MORE ON ALDI

The Seedtastic bread comes across as healthy because it contains organic whole grains and seeds.

However, there are five grams of added sugar in every slice, as revealed on the packaging by the TikToker.

2. Southern Grove Dried Mediterranean Apricots

Many consider apricots to be healthy considering their enticing vitamin and mineral content.

But they also contain potassium sorbate and sulfur dioxide.

3. Simply Nature Original Beef Jerkey

If you take a look at the packaging, the contents of grass-fed beef will stand out to most consumers.

But you need to know that it includes natural flavors and yeast extract.

4. Simply Nature Organic Blueberry Preserves

Fruits can be sugary, but this item goes a step further.

Aldi's Organic Blueberry Preserves includes 13 grams of sugar per serving.

That's higher than what is in a traditional blueberry, the food expert said.

5. Simply Nature Balsamic Vinegar

Balsamic vinegar is touted by consumers for having low fat and little sugar.

However, the TikToker said that the Simply Nature-branded product contains "highly inflammatory soybean oil."

6. Health-Ade Kombucha

Generally, Health-Ade Kombucha is reportedly good for supplementing your immune system.

Aldi's product contains a "whopping" 12 grams of sugar, according to the TikToker.
