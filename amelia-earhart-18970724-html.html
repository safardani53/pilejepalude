Amelia Earhart (Pilot) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Amelia Mary Earhart is an American aviation pioneer and author. Earhart was the first female pilot to cross the Atlantic solo. She set several other records, wrote bestsellers about her flying experiences, and helped found The Ninety-Nines, an organization for women pilots. Born in Atchison, Kansas, Earhart developed a passion for adventure from a young age, constantly gaining flying experience in his twenties. In 1928, Earhart became the first female passenger to cross the Atlantic by plane, becoming a celebrity. In 1932, flying the Lockheed Vega 5B, Earhart completed a nonstop alone transatlantic flight, becoming the first woman to achieve such a feat. She received the Flying Cross Award awarded by the United States for this achievement. In 1935 Earhart became a visiting lecturer at Purdue University as an aeronautical engineering consultant and career advisor to female students.
