Austin Dillon: Professional Career

The Ace started his professional career as a racing car driver in 2005. He started his career with the Bandolero Mini Cars and Legend Cars Series.

In 2008, he joined the Camping World East Series. After four races with the team, Dillon joined his grandfather’s Richard Childress Racing banner.

After that, Austin joined the first Camping World Truck Series driving the No. 3 truck. He finished the 2010 season with two wins and seven poles, earning him the rookie of the year honor.

Likewise, for the 2012 season, he competed in the Sprint Cup Series, driving the No. 33 Chevrolet for Richard Childress Racing.

Also, in August 2012, Dillon participated in one race in the NASCAR Canadian Tire Series, driving the No. 4 car for Fitzpatrick Motorsports.

For the 2015 season, while participating in the Coke Zero 400, The Ace suffered from an injury after his car hit the front tire. He suffered from a bruised tailbone and a bruise on his forearm from this accident.

In the Infinity series 2016, he completed the series by splitting the No. 2 RCR along with six other drivers. Similarly, he scored third position at the Talladega Cup race, one of his best career scores.

The following year, Dillon became the commenter on Fox NASCAR‘s broadcast of the Xfinity Series race at Michigan.

In 2018, the racing car driver got the chance to drive the No. 10 Chevrolet for Kaulig Racing at Indianapolis.

Sadly Austin tested positive for Covid-19, which led him to miss the Go Bowling 235 at Daytona. For the 2021 season, he joined RWR-Eurasia Motorsport.
