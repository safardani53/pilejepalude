Bar Rescue recap Jon Taffer slams Ed as a 'deadbeat' and 'lousy husband' as team fails stress te

JON Taffer did not mince words when addressing the owner of the Cork and Keg during Sunday's episode of Bar Rescue.

After sending two of his friends in for recon and watching how poorly the bar was run Taffer confronted owner, Ed.

Jon tells Ed he doesn’t know anything and he doesn’t even know what the beer cost is and he didn’t even look to find out.

He continues and says he is going to take his wife off a cliff and calls him a "lousy f***king husband and a deadbeat."

During the stress test, the staff fails to make it past 30 minutes and Jon forces Ed to apologize to the paying customers.

But Jon sits down with Ed and his wife Valerie and explains to him that he was being hard on him because he believes in him.

Jon renovates the bar and changes the name to Valerie's Brewhouse, after six weeks the bar has increased sales by $1,500 and opened later to accommodate more customers.

  • The Bar Rescue schedule

    Bar Rescue shoots for five days, on the first day, Jon arrives and does the recon.

    Day two is the most brutal day for Bar Rescue because that’s the day of the stress test which starts early in the day and last until about 3 or 4 in the morning.

    Day three is all about the training where the experts go in and teach the staff how to bartend, cook, and any other skills the staff needs training on.

    Day four is transformation day when the bar is remodeled inside and outside.

    The final day is the big reveal where the staff is shown the changes around sundown, and then the bar opens to the public, according to jontaffer.com

  • Advice for bartenders trying to get into the industry

    In an interview on jontaffer.com the bar connoisseur provides advice for future bartenders.

    He said: "If you want to learn to be a GREAT bartender, then you have to work in a place with a great cocktail program. Work for a bar and gain experience!"

    He continued: "Work for places you could REALLY learn from and from people you REALLY look up to – only work for those who you want to emulate."

  • Taffer picks wisely

    Jon Taffer picks experts based on their abilities and their capacities (the foods, beverages, and products that they know)

    The expert chosen also depends on the kind of bar he is trying to rescue.

    When possible Jon tries to bring in new experts – particularly new chefs & mixologists to keep things moving around, according to jontaffer.com
