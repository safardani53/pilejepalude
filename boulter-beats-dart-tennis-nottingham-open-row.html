Fuming British tennis star slams her own friend's 'unprofessional gesture' in awkward row just weeks

TENNIS pals Katie Boulter and Harriet Dart locked horns after their quarter-final in Nottingham - just weeks before the Brits will cheer each other on at Wimbledon. 

It is thought the row was sparked when British No1 Boulter pointed to her head as she celebrated a 6-3 7-5 win over her friend.

🇬🇧 Katie Boulter is through to the semi-finals of the Nottingham Open after beating fellow Brit Harriet Dart.

They didn't have the most friendly handshake at the net afterwards 👀 #BBCTennis pic.twitter.com/buzs9vPlKw

As the duo shook hands, fuming Dart pointed to her own head and said: “I know you’re doing it towards me. It’s not professional.” 

To which Boulter hit back, furiously shaking her head, saying: “It’s nothing personal. Mate, I do it every single match. Every match."

When asked about Harriet in her post match interview the 26-year-old did not address the incident but said: “It’s awful playing a friend. 

“She’s such a tough competitor I knew it was going to be an absolute battle.

“It took a while to get over it but it was a really tough battle and an incredible achievement by both of us to be here.”
