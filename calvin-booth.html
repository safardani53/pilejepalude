Calvin Booth | NBA Career

Starting from Calvin’s early 20s, Calvin was named the Big Ten Defensive Player of the Year. Moreover, he was a second-team All-Big Ten Conference pick.

Meanwhile, in 1999, Booth was drafted for the second round of the NBA draft by the Washington Wizards, and he immediately signed a multi-year contract with the team.

Consequently, he played for the Wizards, the Dallas Mavericks, Philadelphia 76ers, Seattle SuperSonics, Milwaukee Bucks, Minnesota Timberwolves, and Sacramento Kings.

In a word, Calvin scored an average of 3.3 points, 2.8 rebounds, and 1.0 blocks per game.

Earlier in the 2001 NBA playoffs against Utah Jazz, Booth maintained a layup with 9.8 seconds to give Dallas a game-ending 84-83, leading 3-2 series victory.

Moreover, this game became Mavericks’ first winning playoff series since the 1988 Western Conference semi-finals against Denver.

Later on 13th January 2004, Calvin made a career-high record of 10 blocks within 17 minutes, playing as a Supersonics member. 

Moreover, on 10th September of 2007, Booth signed with the Philadelphia 76ers as a free agent.
