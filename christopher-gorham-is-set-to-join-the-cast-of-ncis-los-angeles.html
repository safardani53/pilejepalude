Christopher Gorham Is Set To Join The Cast Of NCIS: Los Angeles

Season 14 of NCIS: Los Angeles welcomes Christopher Gorham to the cast. He will allegedly portray Alex, the estranged son of retired Admiral Hollace Kilbride (Gerald McRaney). Although the host network, CBS, has not commented, Gorham posted a publication rumor about his casting on Instagram, thus confirming the news. The Lincoln Lawyer actor uploaded a selfie of himself with assistant director Melissa Feuchtinger with a copy of the story.

Christopher Gorham’s previous acting efforts and voice parts

Christopher Gorham made his acting debut in 1989 with Baywatch and went on to appear in other sci-fi films over the next several years. Among his early works are Odyssey 5, Jake 2.0, Party of Five, Popular, Medical Investigation, Relative Chaos, and Felicity. He appeared as the male protagonist in The Other Side of Heaven, co-starring Oscar winner Anne Hathaway, in 2001. He received the Camie Award for his performance.

Further, Christopher Gorham rose to notoriety and was nominated for a Screen Actors Guild Award for his performance as Henry Grubstick in the Silvio Horta-created ABC sitcom Ugly Betty. Covert Affairs, in which he portrayed blind special operations operative Auggie Anderson, was his next major assignment. From July 13, 2010, through December 18, 2014, the 48-year-old actor appeared on the program. The California native has had pivotal parts in Netflix’s Insatiable and The Lincoln Lawyer. In the latter, he played Trevor Elliott, a rich video game creator accused of double murder. Christopher Gorham has provided the voice of Barry Allen aka Flash in six DC Animated Movie Universe films. His voice performances started with Justice League: War (2014) and ended with Justice League Dark: Apokolips War (2020).

Christopher Gorham’s feature directorial debut in 2017 and forthcoming projects

With We Love You, Sally Carmichael!, the actor made the leap to feature film director. On August 4, 2017, it had a limited theatrical premiere, and a video was published four months later. Christopher told the press at the time that he liked directing his first feature picture. He went on to say:

“I like directing. I like teamwork and companionship. It’s really satisfying to take a story and help bring it to life. We filmed the whole picture in 13 days, which were some of the most enjoyable days of my career.”

Aside from his forthcoming role in NCIS: Los Angeles season 14, Christopher Gorham is working on the DC animated film Batman: The Doom That Came to Gotham and the TV series Unplanned in Akron.

He would play Oliver Queen or Green Arrow in Batman: The Doom That Came to Gotham. Meanwhile, in Unplanned in Akron, he will play Jeff, a kind and supportive parent. On February 19, 2023, NCIS: Los Angeles returns with its 12th episode, In the Name of Honor. All 14 seasons are available on Amazon, Apple TV, Vudu, Google Play, and Microsoft.
