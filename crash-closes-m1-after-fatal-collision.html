Multiple vehicle crash closes M1 just hours after fatal collision with three crashes on carria

PART of the M1 motorway has been closed following a multiple vehicle crash - the third major incident on the motorway in 24 hours.

West Yorkshire Police said part of the southbound carriageway from junction 40 to 39 was closed following the incident at around 7.45pm.

"It is expected to be closed for several hours. Please avoid the area if you can,” said the force.

Highways England said the crash happened on a section of the road at Durkar, near Wakefield.

The incident follows a fatal collision 140 miles further south in Bedfordshire, late on Christmas Eve.

A woman, who has not been named, was pronounced dead at the scene following the crash.

The incident happened between Aylesbury and Flitwick on the northbound carriageway at about 11.15pm and involved a number of vehicles.

Another person suffered minor injuries, police said.

A stretch of the motorway was closed from Tuesday night until Wednesday morning after the crash near junction 12.

Drivers were left stranded for several hours into the early hours of Christmas Day following the crash.

A second accident took place on the M1 in Hertfordshire, between Luton Airport and Dunstable.

Highways England described it as a "serious collision".

The M1 fully reopened in both directions before 9am on Christmas Day.
