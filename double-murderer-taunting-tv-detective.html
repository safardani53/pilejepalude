Double-murder suspect appears to be taunting the TV detective who is looking for him

ONE of Britain’s most wanted men is apparently taunting a TV detective who is hunting him.

Kevin Parle, 38, has been on the run for 14 years after two shooting murders in Merseyside.

Ex-Scotland Yard cop Peter Bleksley, the Chief on Channel 4 show Hunted, launched a bid to find Parle last month.

Since then a LinkedIn page has been set up in Parle’s name, saying he is a mechanic living in Spain.

Bleksley was added as a connection and a message was sent to him saying: “Keep up the good work Peter xx.”

The detective said: “He’s watching my every move now he knows I’m coming for him. I just replied ‘thank you Kevin Parle’, but didn’t put any kisses.

Kevin Parle, 38, has been on the run for 14 years after the chilling murders of a mum Lucy Hargreaves, 22, in 2005 and 16-year-old Liam Kelly the year before.

Liam was shot in the chest with a shotgun as he got out of a friend’s Vauxhall Astra in Toxteth, Liverpool, in June 2004.

Lucy was gunned down by masked gunmen as she slept on a sofa in her home in Walton, Merseyside, as her toddler and partner slept upstairs in August 2005.

Lucy’s building was torched but her partner Gary Campbell – believed to have been the intended target – escaped with the tot through an upstairs window.

Parle fled to Alicante, Spain, following the second killing and is on Britain’s most wanted list in connection with both murders.

The LinkedIn profile only has Bleksley as a “connection” and appears to have been set up purely to message him.
