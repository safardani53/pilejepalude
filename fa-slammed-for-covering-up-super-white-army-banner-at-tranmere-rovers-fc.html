FA slammed for covering up 'Super White Army' banner at Tranmere Rovers FC

A BANNER reading "Super White Army" was covered up by the FA during an England women's football match amid claims it was racist.

The supporters' slogan — referring to Tranmere Rovers FC's shirt colour — had been in the stands at the club's Prenton Park ground for months.

But officials decided to cover it up ahead of the Lionesses' televised 6-0 win over Russia in a World Cup qualifier on Tuesday.

Hours earlier England player Lucy Bronze had posted a picture of the banner on Instagram as the squad trained on the pitch before kick-off.

The FA confirmed it took down the banner but said it would not comment further.

But the decision was slammed by fans who claimed it was because it could be seen as racist.

One said on the Total Tranmere forum: “The FA had the SWA banner remove on Tuesday night because of implications of what the banner may have stood for.

“Bloody daft, everyone who would watched the match most likely would have some knowledge that Tranmere fans are known as the SWA”

Mark Bartley, chairman of the Tranmere Rovers Supporters' Club, said the banner had been on display at the ground since the summer, but the words had "been our motto for years".

He said: "It is not remotely racist - it is simply a reference to the white shirts that our team wear.

We are proud of our club colours, as are all fans up and down the country... and will continue to use the chant at our games going forward."

Tranmere Rovers FC declined to comment, according to the BBC.

The day after the match, coach Mark Sampson was sacked over allegations of inappropriate conduct in his previous job at Bristol City.
