Nancy Cordes great qualifications

Although Cordes was born in L.A., she spent her childhood on the islands of Kauai, Hawaii. She went to Punahou School located in Honolulu and graduated from University of Pennsylvania in 1995. 

After that, Cordes went to Princeton University's Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs from where she got her master's degree in public policy.

Nancy Cordes early career

Cordes began her career as a reporter before she finished her master’s degree, that is, in 1995. She worked for KHNL-TV in Honolulu from 1995 to 1997. In 1999, she worked for WJLA-TV located in Washington D.C.

During her time in WJLA-TV, Cordes covered series of historical events including the 9/11 attack, the presidential race of 2000, Bosnia’s peacekeeping efforts, and so on. She left the channel in 2003.

In 2003, Cordes joined NewsOne as its Washington-based correspondent. NewsOne was ABC News’ affiliate news service. While her time in NewsOne she covered various events including the 2004 election. She joined ABC in 2005.
