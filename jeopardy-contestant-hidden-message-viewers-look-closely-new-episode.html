Jeopardy! contestant reveals hidden message viewers can see if they 'look closely' during new episod

A JEOPARDY! contestant has revealed a hidden message that viewers can spot in a recent episode.

On December 29th, Jeopardy! champ Ray LaLonde faced off against Rachel Cohen, a consultant from Denver, Colorado, and Emily Kawaler, a bioinformatician from New York.

Emily shared an inside tidbit from the game show in her "postmortem thread" on Twitter. 

During the installment, Ray mistakenly answered the Daily Double question when he should have said: "Who is Henry David Thoreau?"

At that moment, Emily had the lead with $200 against Ray and Rachel, who both had a balance of zero dollars.

The bioinformatician had a smile on her face and seemed to have said something as her mic was silenced.

KEN'S TENURE

Ken Jennings, 48, served as host of the show until January- his co-star, Mayim Bialik, 47, has been hosting the spinoff Celebrity Jeopardy! but will take over for the former champ in 2023.

Over the summer, Ken and Mayim were officially announced as the permanent replacements for the late Alex Trebek, who had hosted the quiz show since 1984.

ALEX'S ACHIEVEMENT

During the final episode of 2022, Ken shared a personal story about Alex with the audience.

Ken mentioned current champ Ray once interviewed the game show legend when Alex was on his way to Ottawa to receive the Order of Canada.

Ray said the Order was "sort of a Canadian knighthood," and honored those who represent Canada the best.

Jeopardy! shared an image of Alex- alongside his wife Jean- wearing the red and white medal he received for the honor.
