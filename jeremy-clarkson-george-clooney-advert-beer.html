Jeremy Clarkson rips into Hollywood star George Clooney in foul-mouthed advert for Hawkstone beer

ONE is a Hollywood A-lister adored by millions... and the other is a foul-mouthed celebrity farm owner.

Now Jeremy Clarkson has playful prodded heartthrob George Clooney in a new advert for his beer, Hawkstone Lager.

Filming inside his home on Diddly Squat Farm, in West Oxfordshire, The Grand Tour host, 62, tells the film star to “f*** off” in a tongue-in-cheek Instagram post.

Jeremy mocked George, who has endorsed Nespresso for years, for encouraging people to buy high-end coffee machines.

He said: “Over the years, hundreds of thousands if not millions of people have been duped by George Clooney into believing they need one of these coffee machines in their kitchen.

“Not so, what you actually need is one of these, a machine which can deliver perfectly-chilled draft Hawkstone lager.”

After pouring himself a pint, Jeremy takes a large swig and smiles at the camera before stating: “F*** off George Clooney!” 

The cheeky clip has received nearly 250,000 likes and was captioned: “The ad’s been banned of course, but you can at least get the machine from beerhawk.co.uk.”

The majority of fans loved the post, describing it as “too bloody funny” and others urged him to become Prime Minister and said they would “not skip commercials like this”.

Jeremy launched Hawkestone Lager last year and by Christmas, it was Britain's bestselling beer on Amazon.

He teamed up with Cotswold Brew Company to create the 4.8 per cent brew but insists it is farm manager Kaleb Cooper, 21, who has made it a success.

Since the beer came out, Jeremy has tried to sneak in cheeky innuendos and outlandish statements into his unique adverts.
