Who is Joe Sugg and what is the YouTube stars net worth? The Sun

JOE Sugg's career soared when he took part in Strictly back in 2018.

After taking over the world of YouTube he is now a big TV star.

Who is Joe Sugg?

Joe Sugg was born in Wiltshire on September 8, 1991, and his sister of fellow YouTuber Zoella.

The YouTube star used to be a roof thatcher prior to his vlogging career, which is where his online name ThatcherJoe came from.

He shot to national stardom when he appeared on Strictly 2018 where he reached the final with partner, and now girlfriend Dianne Buswell.

He stands at around 5ft 8in tall.

What is Joe Sugg's YouTube channel?

With three different channels, Joe, who began YouTube in 2011, makes videos on varying topics which has almost 8million subscribers.

His main channel, known as ThatcherJoe, for the most part, comprises of challenges, pranks, and impressions.

Titles of his well-known videos include Ultimate Roommate Revenge Pranks! and Singing Impressions with Conor Maynard, a video in which the YouTube star is joined by pop singer Conor Maynard.

However, he has also branched out and also has a daily video diary channel, ThatcherJoeVlogs, and a gaming channel ThatcherJoeGames that both also have millions of subscribers.

Are Joe Sugg and Dianne Buswell still dating?

As of January 2023 the couple are still going strong.

They regularly post loved up selfies and they also live together.

They got together after meeting on Strictly in 2018, but only came clean with their romance after the show was over.

Joe and Dianne shared stunning snaps of Joe's surprise 30th birthday in a vineyard earlier this month.

What is Joe Sugg's net worth?

As of 2023 Joe's fortune is reckoned by to be in the region of £8million.

His successful YouTube channel is his primary source of income, as he earns money through advertising revenue.

But he is also paid for some of his guest appearances and cameo work.

What other work has Joe Sugg done?

Much like many of his fellow YouTubers, Joe's work is not limited to video-making.

In 2015, Joe appeared in the film Joe and Caspar Hit the Road alongside flatmate and YouTuber Caspar Lee.

The pair also had cameo voiceover roles as seagulls in the recent cartoon The SpongeBob Movie: Sponge Out of Water.

Aside from films, Joe Sugg is the author of the graphic novel Username: Evie that was published in 2015.

In 2020 he starred in West End show The Waitress

Joe also had a part of the cast of BBC One's The Syndicate.
