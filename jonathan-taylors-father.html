Who Is Jonathan Taylor’s Father, Jonathan James?

Jonathan James is a former basketball player who got wide fame as the father of NFL’s Indianapolis Colts player Jonathan Taylor.

James was born in late 1960 in the United States. He is in his 60s as of November 2023.

Taylor’s father is an alumnus of San Fransisco State University- he played basketball for the Gators from 1982 to 1986. But he never made up to the NBA.

Parents Of Jonathan Taylor Were Never Married

The NFL running back Taylor is the son of Jonathan James and Elizabeth Taylor. His parents never married and separated after his birth (b. January 19, 1999, Salem, New Jersey).

Jonathan Taylor was raised by his single mother, Elizabeth, with four older siblings. As a result, he uses his mom’s maiden name ‘Taylor.’

As reported, Elizabeth Taylor worked as a medical technology staff at a local hospital. She even worked during night shifts to support the family.

Despite not living with Elizabeth, Jonathan James co-raised his son Jonathan Taylor with her.

Is Jonathan Taylor’s Father Jonathan James Close With Him?

Yes, he and his father are close. James has remained by Taylor’s side since his childhood. Moreover, Jonathan James played a crucial role in his upbringing, inspiring him to become a professional athlete.

As per online sources, he was not as strict as Taylor’s mother, Elizabeth- but would find ways to encourage him to enjoy time on the fields rather than playing Xbox games.

His parents played an instrumental role in making Jonathan what he is today.

Brief On NFL Player Jonathan Taylor

The 2-time Doak Walker Award winner at college was NCAA’s sixth-leading rusher all-time. He was the only athlete to dash for more than 6,000 yards in three years.

After skipping his senior year at the University of Wisconsin–Madison, Jonathan Taylor became the 41st pick of the Indianapolis Colts in the 2020 NFL Draft.

He became one of four players from Wisconsin eligible in the draft; the other three were Jonah Jackson, Tyler Biadasz, and Jake Fromm.

He joined Michael Pittman Jr. (WR), Julian Blackmon (S), Jacob Eason (QB), and Danny Pinter (G) as a rookie.

Other notable players in the 2020 NFL Draft were Joe Burrow, Chase Young, Jeff Okudah, Andrew Thomas, Justin Herbert, and Derrick Brown, among others.

In his rookie season (2020-21), Jonathan started in 13 out of 15 game appearances. He rushed 1169 yards and got 11 receiving touchdowns with 36 receptions.

The 2021-22 season (the second of his four-year deal) saw him a career-high 1811 rushing yards while making 18 rushing touchdowns on 40 receptions.
