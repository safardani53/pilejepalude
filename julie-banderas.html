Who is Julie Banderas?

Julie Banderas is a popular American television news anchor for the Fox News Channel based in New York City. She was born Julie Bidwell but later took the professional name, Banderas.

The anchor has hosted Fox Report Weekend.

Afterward, she got a weekday anchor role and now serves as a primary weekday fill-in anchor on programs such as America’s Newsroom, The Faulkner Focus, talk show Outnumbered, and a guest host on The Big Saturday/Sunday Show.

Banderas is also a regular guest on Fox News’s late-night comedy show Gutfeld! aka The Great Gutfeld Show. Let’s discover her complete journey.

Early Life of Julie Banderas

Julie Banderas was born on 25 September 1973 in Hartford, Connecticut, United State of America. She is the second daughter of her father Howard D. Bidwell and her mother Fabiola R, who was a Colombian.

Her father was a Navy veteran and civil engineer who started a company called Consolidated Precast, Inc.

Banderas’s sister’s name is Melissa with whom she grew up. Further, she has four half-siblings from her father’s first marriage.

The journalist had developed a keen interest in journalism since her early days. Her father enhanced him to pursue her career in the journalism field.

Following this, she enrolled at Emerson College and earned a bachelor’s degree in broadcast journalism in 1996.

Julie Banderas Career

Julie Banderas started her career at a television station at WLVI-TV located in Boston. Later, she served as a local news anchorwoman for WHSV-TV in Harrisonburg, Virginia.

She then moved to serve as a local news anchor for WBRE-TV in Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania. And then, at WFSB-TV in Hartford, Connecticut, and then at WNYW in New York City.

Doing these jobs gained her great experience and helped her to build a winning resume. Looking at her skills, she then got selected for Fox News in March 2005 as a general assignment reporter.

But Banderas was not going to stop here.

Her so cool thoughts and aptitudes won the director’s heart and she eventually became the anchor for Fox Report Weekend, replacing Laurie Dhue in 2008.

In 2010, Banderas took maternity leave with Harris Faulkner to fulfill her parental responsibilities. By the end of the same year, she came back to Fox News and was reassigned to the general news correspondent position.
