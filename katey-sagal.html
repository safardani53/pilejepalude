Katey Sagal Biography

Catherine Louise aka Katey Sagal was born in Los Angeles, California on January 19, 1954. She is currently 69 years old. Her mother, Sara Zwilling, and father, Boris Sagal, gave birth to her. Her mother was a well-known singer who could perform English, Scottish, and German songs. She was also the first female director in Hollywood.

Her mother died in Los Angeles, California, on September 1, 1975. Similarly, her father is a director who has worked on films such as The Omega Man. Liz and Jean Sagal, Joey Sagal, Gregg Champion, and David Sagal are her siblings. She also studied at the California Institute of Arts.

Career

Katey Sagal began her career as a singer with the band The Group With No Name. Later, in 1973, she appeared as a backing vocalist for Bob Dylan and Tanya Tucke. She supported vocalists such as Olivia Newton-John. Finally, in June 2004, she released her first solo album, ‘Well…,’ followed by ‘Room.’ From 1985 to 1986, she played a newspaper journalist in a Chicago newspaper office in the serial ‘Mary.’

She was cast as Peggy Bundy in the sitcom “Married… with Children.” Furthermore, the series lasted ten years (1987–1997). Moving on, Peg Bundy rose to prominence during this time period. As her initial success grew, she began to receive offers for television shows. As a result, she appeared in many more television films and appeared as a guest star. She provided her voice for ‘Captain Leela’ in the science fiction comedy ‘Futurama.’ She appeared as a guest on three episodes of That ’70s Show. Moving on, in the year 2000, she appeared on NBC in the sitcom ‘Tucker.’ She portrayed John Ritter’s wife in 8 Simple Rules for Dating My Teenage Daughter. Furthermore, Ritter died after three episodes of the second season, forcing the show to be canceled.

From 2005 to 2008, she appeared in the serials ‘Lost,’ ‘The Winner,’ and ‘Eli Stone.’ Her breakthrough came from roles in the films ‘House Broken’ and ‘Sons of Anarchy.’ Similarly, she portrayed Gemma Teller Morrow in the world of California biker gangs. In September 2014, she was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. In addition, in 2016, she joined the cast of the popular sitcom ‘The Big Bang Theory’ as Penny’s mother.

From 2017 to 2018, she starred in the CBS sitcom Superior Donuts. She then had a recurring role in the ABC television series ‘The Conners’ in 2018 and 2019. In 2021, she portrayed Annie Bello in the ABC television series Rebel. She has also appeared in Made to Order as Louise, Smart House, Recess: School’s Out, and Futurama.
