Masked gang who posed with their arsenal of weapons and snorted cocaine off a shotgun are jailed

SHOCKING pictures show a gang of masked gun toting thugs showing off their weapons weeks before they were caged.

The youths who "thought that they were untouchable" were even seen snorting drugs from the barrel of a sawn-off shotgun, before they were jailed this week for a string of gun offences.

Adam Mercer was filmed snorting the class a drug off the barrel of a sawn-off shotgun

DNA, photos and videos were used to convict all three of serious offences.

Adam Mercer, of Cullen Avenue, Bootle, Liverpool, can be seen in a video recorded weeks before he was jailed lowering his head down to the weapon on a bed and inhaling white powder.

After this he is heard to shout "oh my god, what the f***?" while people in the room with him call him "sick".

The gang were no strangers to posing for pictures and videos with weapons and drugs.

They were previously pictured in a sinister shot wearing a V For Vendetta-style Guy Fawkes mask while holding two shotguns.

In another picture he poses for, he is seen sat on a bike while wearing a helmet and balaclava and holding the two shotguns out in front of him.

Mercer was sentenced on Monday to three years in jail for affray, and possession of a firearm without a firearms certificate. He had previously pleading guilty.

The 22-year-old was jailed at Liverpool Crown Court along with Malcolm Graham, 19, of Albert Road, Southport, Merseyside.

Graham was filmed firing the pistol that later killed 17-year-old Kelvin Wilson after he was shot in the head in February this year.

Mercer was handed a three-year sentence and Graham was locked up for 11 years while a third offender, Lewis Clarke, 18, of Southport Road, Formby, Merseyside, was jailed for seven-and-a-half years for firearm offences on Tuesday.

No one has ever been convicted of killing Mr Wilson near Smithdown Road in Liverpool last year.

Graham had earlier admitted charges of possessing a prohibited firearm, possession of a firearm without a firearms certificate and two counts of Section 20 wounding.

The first attack was on a prison inmate while the second attack was on Mercer after a disagreement.

Clarke had also pleading guilty to possessing the Baikal self-loading pistol, possession of ammunition without a firearms certificate and possession of a firearm with intent to cause fear of violence.

He also admitted possession of ammunition with intent to cause fear of violence after he fired a blank firing pistol which contained a homemade bullet during a dispute at a friend's house in Gardner Road, Formby on May 14 last year.

Following the sentencing, Merseyside Police Detective Inspector Richie Jones, said: "The sentencing of these three offenders today sends out a strong message to those who are involved in gun crime - Merseyside Police is committed to taking those involved in gun crime off the streets.

"Malcolm Graham and Lewis Clark were both linked by DNA, photographs and video to a weapon which just three weeks before it came in to their possession was used to murder Kevin Wilson on Smithdown Road in February 2015.

"These three thought that they were untouchable and were seen in photographs on phones owned by Graham and Clarke with the weapons. Mercer was also videoed on the phone snorting cocaine off a sawn-off shotgun.

"People like Malcolm Graham, and those he associates with, have a complete disregard for each other, as you can see from the assault on Mercer, let alone other members of the public.

"They use fear, violence and intimidation to get what they want and think they can do what they want without any come back. They couldn't be more wrong."
