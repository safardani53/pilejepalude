He has shared that he is “heartbroken” as his name has been withdrawn from the squad of World Cup Qatar 2022.

Earlier, he played for Major League Soccer club D.C. United.

He also featured for IMG Soccer Academy, Arsenal FC, and LA Galaxy as a youth. Later, as a senior, Paul played for Tijuana as well.

Paul Arriola | Early Life

Paul (fully named Paul Joseph Arriola) was born on February 5, 1995, under Aquarius’ sun sign. He was born in Chula Vista, California, U.S.

His parents are Dawn Arriola and Art Arriola. Additionally, he has a younger brother.

Arriola is of Mexican descent, as his great-grandparents immigrated from Mexico to the United States. 

As for his academic background, Arriola first attended the Mater Dei Catholic High School in Chula Vista, CA., and then iHigh Virtual Academy in San Diego.

After this, Arriola took classes at Southwestern College.

Paul Arriola | Soccer Career

Paul Arriola commenced playing ever since his high school days. Initially, he was featured for the IMG Soccer Academy.

Following it, he played for the U.S. Soccer Development Academy club Arsenal FC based in Temecula, California. 

During his tenure, he received a professional contract from the Galaxy. However, he opted to play for the Club Tijuana of Liga MX. 

“I felt that Xolos was the place for me. I love the club; it’s my home team, and the people down there are wonderful.”
-Paul Arriola

On July 6, 2013, Arriola made his club debut against Club América at Petco Park in San Diego. Later, he joined the Los Angeles Galaxy. 

Tijuana

On July 19, 2013, Arriola made his professional league debut against Club Atlas to open the 2013–14 Liga MX season.

He played as a second-half substitute during his tenure while assisting Darío Benedetto’s goal. Later, he made another goal in CONCACAF Champions League against C.D. Victoria.

D.C. United 

Arriola signed a deal with the Major League Soccer side D.C. United on August 9, 2017.

To give his rights from the Galaxy to D.C. United, they paid the club fee of $3,000,000 with added allocation money for his rights.

Advancing on August 13, he made the club debut over Real Salt Lake; however, his foremost goal only came against the New York Red Bulls.

To illustrate, his goal was on October 22, 2017, in RFK Stadium. 

During his first season with the team, Arriola made 11 appearances, two assists, and only one goal.

Following the season, he improved the season with his own two scores at Audi Field. He ended with 28 appearances, seven goals, and eight assists.

He also starred as both a right midfielder and right back and played with D.C. United.

During the 2020 pre-season, he appeared in a game against Orlando City but had an injury at that time.

He returned to the field from an injury in D.C. United’s last regular season game, which finished in a 2-3, Arriola was loaned out to Swansea City.

Afterward, he first played for Swansea on February 1, 2021, against Manchester City. He was set to play for the 2020–21 EFL Championship season.

Due to his injury, his loan gameplay with the team ended on March 31, 2021. In January 2022, FC Dallas acquired the athlete in an MLS-record trade from DC United.

International Team 

Paul Arriola made his international game debut during the 2011 FIFA U-17 World Cup in Mexico. In Mexico, he made three starts in four matches.

Indeed, he was also a part of the 2011 CONCACAF U-17 Championship; however, he didn’t appear in any of its games due to his injury. 

During his tenure as a United States U18 squad member, he requested Mexican citizenship.

To elaborate, it was the friendlies match against France and Uruguay, and he chose a domestic spot rather than the foreign player spot.

Likewise, he has played at the 2014 Dallas Cup, the 2014 NTC Invitational, and the 2015 FIFA U-20 World Cup. After playing with the United States U20 squad, he played for the United States U23 squad. 

As a youth, he last played at Toyota Stadium in Frisco, Texas. Not to mention, he made his senior debut against Puerto Rico on May 22, 2016. 

Furthermore, he played in the 2019 CONCACAF Gold Cup in Saint Paul, Minnesota.

In 2021-2022 CONCACAF World Cup qualifying, he scored a header in USMNT’s penultimate game, where the USMNT defeated Panama 5-1, virtually guaranteeing a place in Qatar.

Sadly, he was not selected for World Cup Qatar 2022.

Paul Arriola | Controversy

Arriola apologized for the offensive tweets he made a decade ago that resurfaced in 2013.

He was playing for Swansea City on loan from D.C. United and expressed he was “ashamed” by the tweets and insisted he has grown as a human since the time he posted the tweets.

The tweets were in one group of screengrabs from 2012 when Arriola was 17 years old and a member of the Los Angeles Galaxy youth academy.

In the tweets, he said disapproval over female color commentators, quotes a Rick Ross lyric containing a racial slur, uses the same racial slur while interacting with a friend, and makes a joke about Black people swimming.

At the time, he was a member of Swansea City; similarly, the club released a statement saying that they did not want their club member to condone any form of discriminatory behavior from anyone associated with their club.

Paul Arriola | Personal Life 

Paul Arriola lost his father, Arturo Arriola III, who passed away due to an apparent heart attack.

According to the sources, his father used to be an alcoholic and was just 48 when he passed away. 

Unfortunately, alcoholism was the same reason why his parents separated. However, he chose to live with his father and often feared alcohol would take his father away from him. 

He said, “It’s a disease. It eats you up. He was good when he wasn’t drinking, and he knew that is how he wanted to be, but when there were situations of stress or unhappiness, it’s very easy to go back to it.”

Apart from his father, Arriola’s paternal great-grandfather also passed away due to cirrhosis of the liver.

Due to all these reasons, Paul Arriola keeps himself away from alcohol.
