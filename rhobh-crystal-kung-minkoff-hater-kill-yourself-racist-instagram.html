REAL Housewives Of Beverly Hills’ Crystal Kung Minkoff put a hater on blast after the troll urged her to “kill herself” in a sickening racist rant. 

The Bravo star shared the shocking message which included several racial slurs on Instagram Thursday but was soon flooded with support from fans and fellow celebs.

The 38-year-old posted a shocking screenshot of an Instagram DM she received this morning from a troll. 

“I’m sure you get these kinds of messages all the time and granted, I don’t know you (nor do I ever want to in any lifetime based on how you represent yourself on national television),” the message began. 

It continued: “You’re the epitome of a moronic ch**k c**t. PLEASE do the world a favor and kill yourself.

“You stupid, worthless nasty a** Asian s***h napkin. I feel sorry for your children… 

“They have to wake up everyday knowing that their mother is a spineless, worthless piece of Asian s**t,” the repulsive DM concluded. 

In response to the hateful words, Crystal fired back: “What I can tell you “Dashie Pants”, is that my children are doing just fine. 

“In fact, they are learning to speak out and stand up against racism. And they will certainly become allies for all races because God knows we need them more than ever based on your Facebook message.”

SUPPORT FROM A FELLOW HOUSEWIFE

She was met with support from many of her followers, including Real Housewives Of New York star Eboni K. Williams. 

The New Yorker said: “You are so loved Sis. These deranged bigots are just mad AF that their days of dominance are coming to an end."

Dorothy Wang wrote: “Though you are strong, brave, and always find the perfect words to say- 

“I am so sorry you have to endure this type of senseless hate! Bravo for always standing up and speaking out. Love you!”

She thanked her supporters following the messages and called to stop Asian hate. 

“I want to say thank you to everyone who has DM’d me. It’s been hundreds. And I read them all. 

“Your support lifts me up and reminds me to keep the conversation going. We will not be silenced. #StopAsianHate,” she wrote in her updated caption. 

BREAKING BOUNDARIES

Earlier this year, Crystal made history when she became the first Asian American cast member to be featured on Real Housewives of Beverly Hills. 

In May, Crystal’s on-camera nemesis, Sutton Stracke, was forced to apologize to her rival after making insensitive comments about race. 

The Southerner received backlash as she compared Asian stereotyping to being called a redneck during a group trip to Lake Tahoe and also said she “doesn’t see color.”

Sutton, 50, took to Twitter to express an apology to her cast mate and her fans as she called her actions “disrespectful."
