Tough Mudder 5k review: Fun, but not tough or muddy

AFTER a few hundred metres, my jog through the park was interrupted by a couple of overhanging walls that required plenty of ungainly scrambling to negotiate.

Managing to swing my leg over without crashing back down to the floor, I hopped to the other side and started thinking that there's definitely easier ways to cure a hangover than running a Tough Mudder, even if it is just 5K.

The popularity of obstacle races shows no sign of decline at the moment.

There’s events based all around the UK almost all year round, from Tough Mudders, to Spartan races, Wolf Runs and more.

And one of the latest offerings on the scene is Tough Mudder’s city-based 5K event.

The shorter event is designed for people who aren’t bothered about trekking into the heart of the countryside on their weekends (to dive into chest-deep mud baths and clamber up slippery hills in the woods).

The Mudder of all fun runs

Instead, the Tough Mudder 5K is a shorter (and less muddy) event that’s touring the UK this year, with locations including London, Bristol, Manchester and Edinburgh.

The mini mudder is based inside the city limits and takes place on Friday nights and Saturday mornings, meaning it’s pretty easy way to start your weekend off with a bang.

I attended the event in Bristol and, upon arriving on Saturday morning, I was greeted by a few hundred sweaty race finishers drinking pints in the sun to the backdrop of pulsing music from the DJ booth. So far, so normal.

After heading through the usual check-in procedure to make sure I knew I was taking my life in my own hands going around the course, I was off to the standby gate where I waited to start my heat.

Looking around, there was a lot more of a mix of participants than your usual obstacle races.

The 5k is not just for the steely-eyed maniacs who are a regular feature at the longer events; there were plenty of people attending who looked like they’d turned up for a regular jog in the park, which in effect, they had.

A quick warm-up and safety talk followed before taking the Tough Mudder pledge, explaining that these events aren’t races, but opportunities to tackle some adversity as a group.

It’s a nice sentiment I thought, as me and my race partner dashed to the front of the pack, determined not to be overtaken by anyone else running in our heat.

We got glorious sunshine for our event, which definitely helped the festival atmosphere, and I was grateful we had avoided a steady drizzle which would have taken some of the gloss off the day.

Before long we started hitting the obstacles, which are the usual combination of high fences to jump over, nets to crawl under and monkey bars to swing beneath.

If you’re 5'10" or above and can do a chin-up, you’ll probably manage most of what’s thrown your way relatively comfortably.

Below that and the teamwork starts to kick in, and you’ll be helping your fellow runners scramble over the high fences and get a helpful boost from other people in your heat.

Either way, you shouldn’t take much more than a hour to get the course done, even if you struggle with a couple of the obstacles.

From what I saw, people are very willing to give each other a hand when the going gets tough, and it’s a much less competitive environment that some of the other events around.

After what felt like not very much time at all, we’d clambered around the final section of the course and we were rewarded with our finishers t-shirts and post-race pints.

I felt a little underwhelmed when we were done, mostly because I wasn’t wet, muddy and gasping for air.

But actually, if you’re willing to not take it too seriously and you run the course as a group, it’s a lot more fun than a standard Tough Mudder race - if a little less rewarding.

There’s no water/mud-based obstacles in the 5k events, which means once you’re finished, you can comfortably chill in the sunshine with a few post-race bevvies and a burger from one of the food stalls.

Tickets for the events are around £39 depending on when you sign up - find them here - which is a little steep in my book, especially when you can do a half Tough Mudder for £59.

But if you’ve got a mixed-ability group of friends, or you’re looking for an ideal team-building activity, the 5k race would be a pretty good shout.
