Two men killed and two more seriously injured in horror crash on M25 in Surrey

TWO men have been killed and two other seriously injured in a horror crash on the M25.

The single-vehicle smash unfolded at around 3am near the Croydon junction with the M23 close to Bletchingley, Surrey.

Tragically two men aged 31 and 28 were declared dead at the scene.

Police confirmed two others were rushed to hospital with serious injuries.

The M25 has been closed anticlockwise between Junction 8 (Redhill) and Junction 6 (Godstone) while officers investigated.

Drivers have been warned to expect delays and seek an alternative route.

Surrey Police said: "Surrey Police are appealing for witnesses to a fatal road traffic collision on the M25 at Bletchingley at around 3am today (23 February).

"The collision, involving one vehicle, took place near the junction with the M23.

"A 31-year-old man and 28-year-old man has sadly died at the scene. Two other men have been taken to hospital with serious injuries."

Anyone with information can call police on 101 quoting PR/P23038218.
