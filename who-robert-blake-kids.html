Who are Robert Blake's kids?

POPULAR 1960s actor Robert Blake passed away on March 9, 2023, at the age of 89.

Prior to his passing, he tied the knot three times and welcomed three kids.

Who are Robert Blake's kids?

Throughout the course of Robert Blake's life, he welcomed three biological children.

During his 20-year marriage to Sondra Blake Kerr, he welcomed daughter Denilah, who is now married to novelist Gregg Hurwitz, and son Noah.

He then welcomed daughter Rose during his second marriage to Bonny Lee Bakley.

While not much is known about his daughters, his son is now known for his career as an actor.

Over the years, he has starred in several television shows and films, including Red Rooms, Harry and the Hendersons, The Wonder Years, and Married with Children.

What was Robert Blake's cause of death?

Blake began his acting career as a child with a lead role in the short film series Our Gang.

He then went on to star in over 150 additional projects before retiring in the late 1990s.

He was arguably best known for his roles in Baretta, Electra Glide in Blue, Lost Highway, and In Cold Blood.

His niece, Noreen Austin, confirmed that he "passed away peacefully surrounded by family" at his Los Angeles home on March 9, 2023.

Austin said that his cause of death was heart disease, according to The Washington Post.

What happened to Robert Blake’s wife Bonny Lee Bakley?

While Blake was used to making headlines, he was not prepared for the early 2000s ones that accused him of murder.

In 2001, Blake's second wife was fatally shot twice while sitting in her husband's car.

When police arrived at the scene, he was reportedly frantic, pacing and vomiting, allegedly inconsolable after the brutal attack.

He claimed that he found his wife in the car but a year later, he was charged with her murder.

Blake spent almost a year in jail before bail was set, but in 2003, he was acquitted of the murder charge due to "unreliable prosecution witnesses."

During a 2005 interview with Vanity Fair, Blake's son revealed that he wasn't invited to attend the trial, adding that his father's situation was "nobody's fault but his."

At this time, it is unclear who killed Bakley.
