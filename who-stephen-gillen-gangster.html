Who is Stephen Gillen? | The Sun

STEPHEN Gillen is a reformed career criminal, who now wants to help others to avoid falling into a life of crime.

Here, we look at his life, and how he was able to turn it around after a difficult start.

Who is Stephen Gillen?

Stephen Gillen is a reformed gangster from London.

He was shipped to Belfast when he was six months old, where he witnessed horrific violence and death at the height of the Troubles.

He moved to East London at the age of 10, and soon after was known as a notorious figure in the criminal underworld, involved in heists and brutal fights.

How long did Stephen Gillen spend in prison?

Gillen said he spent more than 18 years of his life behind bars.

He was sentenced to 17 years in prison in 1991 for his involvement in a crime operation that was thwarted by the Metropolitan Police.

Gillen was only 22 years old when he got sent down.

When inside, Gillen was a category A prisoner and shared a prison with notorious criminal Charles Bronson, who he befriended whilst inside.

He was moved 25 times whilst behind bars before eventually being released in 2003.

He spent a total of 11 years and nine months inside during that particular sentence.

What does Stephen Gillen do now?

After his release, Gillen decided to turn his life around and became a successful author and TV personality.

He promotes peace and wants to tell his story in order to stop others from going down the path he did.

His website promotes the Resilience Code, which encourages people to overcome adversity and difficulties in their life in order to achieve success.

His book The Monkey Puzzle Tree, which details his upbringing and his entrance into the world of crime, is set to be turned into a big-budget feature film.

The film, written by former Poirot actor David Suchet's son Kieran, has a budget of £30million.

 He is an International Peace Prize Nominee for his work in preventing violence and was even flown to New York to meet with the secretary-general of the United Nations.
